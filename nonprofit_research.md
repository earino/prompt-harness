# Nonprofit Sector Research Summary

## Key Findings (November 2025)

- 58% of nonprofits use AI for daily operations
- 40% lack AI-educated staff
- 95% of nonprofit leaders experience burnout
- Small nonprofits adopt AI at half the rate of larger organizations
- 74% of donors support nonprofit AI use for marketing and operations

## Target User Personas

### Development Director (Fundraising Focus)
- Manages 100-2000 donors solo or with small team
- Constant backlog of donor communications
- Needs personalization at scale
- Limited time, high expectations
- Primary pain: Donor stewardship is time-consuming

### Communications Manager (Content Creation Focus)
- Responsible for 4-5 social platforms
- Weekly newsletter, blog, press releases
- Solo or one assistant
- Needs consistent brand voice across channels
- Primary pain: Content creation from scratch takes too long

### Program Manager (Service Delivery Focus)
- 2-10 program staff or volunteers
- Focus on service delivery, not paperwork
- Required reporting takes time away from programs
- Volunteer coordination is manual
- Primary pain: Grant reporting deadlines conflict with program work

## Tone and Voice Guidance

- Professional but not corporate
- Warm and approachable, not hokey
- Mission-focused, not transactional
- Sector-appropriate language
- Accessible to beginners with limited AI experience
